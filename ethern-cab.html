WHAT ARE THE TWO DIFFERENT TYPES OF ETHERNET CABLE

What are the two different types of Ethernet Cable?

magine Ethernet cables like telephone lines connecting different devices so they can "talk" to each other over a network. There are two main types: straight-through and crossover cables, and they’re wired differently because they’re used for different situations.

 

STRAIGHT THROUGH CABLES

A straight-through cable is the most common type of Ethernet cable, where the wiring on one end of the cable is identical to the wiring on the other end. This is commonly used for connecting different types of devices.

Typical Uses:

  • Connecting a computer to a switch, hub, or router.
  • Connecting a router to a modem.
  • Connecting different types of network devices (e.g., a PC to a network switch)


Computer to router



CROSSOVER CABLES

A crossover cable has different wiring on each end, with specific pairs of wires "crossed" so that the transmit and receive signals are swapped. This allows similar devices to communicate directly with each other without a switch or router.

Typical Uses:

  • Connecting two computers directly to each other.
  • Connecting two network switches directly.
  • Connecting two routers directly (in certain configurations).


Computer to computer

Patterns of Ethernet Cables

STRAIGHT THROUGH CABLE

Straight through cables typically follows TIA/EIA-568B on both ends. The wire arrangement is the same on both sides.

Pin Configuration (TIA/EIA-568B Standard for both ends):
  • Orange-White

  • Brown


CROSSOVER CABLE

Crossover cable is when one end is wired using T568A, and the other end is wired using T568B. The transmit and receive wires are crossed.

TIA/EIA-568A
